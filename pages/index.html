Project E-30

Are you ready to take a trip down memory lane and relive the glory days of the 80s? Look no further than the BMW E30, the second-generation 3-series that was produced from 1982 to 1994. This car is the epitome of what it means to be a true driver's machine. When the E30 was first released, it was clear that BMW had hit the nail on the head with its design. The upgraded suspension and cutting-edge technology helped the car avoid the typical 80s styling, making it a timeless classic. With a variety of body styles to choose from, including the coupe, convertible, wagon, and sedan, there was something for everyone. But it's not just the design that made the E30 stand out. This car was a true performer, with a variety of trims and sub-models, including the 316, 318, 323, and the highly coveted M3. The M3 in particular was a race-winning machine, taking home over 1,400 wins during its production run. It's no wonder that it's still considered to be one of the most successful racing platforms of all time. Driving an E30 is an experience like no other. The car is incredibly nimble, with an emphasis on the driving experience. It's the kind of car that makes you want to hit the open road and push it to its limits. Even though BMW has moved on from the E30 design, the cult following for this car has never died. It's still considered by many to be the ultimate European driving machine. With a 12-year production run and over 2.5 million units made, the E30 is a common sight on roads and tracks around the world. If you're a fan of European cars, or just appreciate a true driver's machine, the E30 is a must-have. So, dust off your leather driving gloves, rev up the engine, and get ready for the ride of your life with the BMW E30.
